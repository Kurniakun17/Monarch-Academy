Civil Engineering

Civil engineering is a field of engineering that deals with the design, construction, and maintenance of infrastructure such as buildings, roads, bridges, and water supply systems. Civil engineers use principles of mathematics, physics, and materials science to create functional and safe structures. The field offers a wide range of career opportunities in both the public and private sectors.

Electronical Engineering

Electrical engineering focuses on electricity, electromagnetism, and electronics. It involves designing, developing, and testing electrical systems and equipment. The major covers topics such as circuit analysis, control systems, digital signal processing, and computer engineering. Electrical engineering offers various career opportunities and plays a crucial role in advancing technology and improving people's quality of life.

Computer Science

Computer science is the study of computers and computational systems, including algorithms, data structures, programming languages, and software design. It involves the use of mathematical, logical, and engineering principles to solve problems and create new technologies. Computer scientists also explore the social and ethical implications of technology, as well as its impact on society.

Bussiness

Business major covers the principles and practices of commerce. It includes topics such as accounting, finance, marketing, and management. The major also covers subjects like business law, organizational behavior, and entrepreneurship. Business graduates have diverse career opportunities in fields such as finance, consulting, and entrepreneurship, driving economic growth and innovation.
